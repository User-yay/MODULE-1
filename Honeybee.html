The Honey bee

The buzz

Honey bees are remarkable social insects renowned for their highly organized colonies and vital role in the global ecosystem. Living in structured hives driven by a complex division of labor, a single colony contains one queen, thousands of female worker bees, and a few hundred male drones. As they forage for nectar and pollen to sustain their hive and produce honey, these industrious insects inadvertently act as primary pollinators for a vast array of flowering plants and agricultural crops. This crucial behavior supports global biodiversity and underpins roughly one-third of the human food supply, making honey bees an indispensable cornerstone of both nature and agriculture.
